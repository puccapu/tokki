Converting Decimal Numbers to Binary and Hexadecimal

Step 1: Convert 99 to Binary

The binary system only uses 0s and 1s. We break down the decimal number 99 by dividing it by 2 and keeping track of the remainders:

99 ÷ 2 = 49 remainder 1

49 ÷ 2 = 24 remainder 1

24 ÷ 2 = 12 remainder 0

12 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 0

6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0

3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1

1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1

Now, we take the remainders and list them from bottom to top.

So, the binary equivalent of 99 is 1100011.

Step 2: Convert 99 to Hexadecimal

The hexadecimal system uses numbers 0–9 and letters A–F. To convert 99 to hexadecimal, we divide it by 16 (since 16 is the base of hexadecimal):

99 ÷ 16 = 6 remainder 3

Since the quotient is 6 (and we can’t divide any further), we’re done!

The quotient is 6, and the remainder is 3.

So, when combined, 99 in hexadecimal is 63.

Final Answer:

99 in binary is 1100011

99 in hexadecimal is 63

Now we know how to convert decimal numbers to binary and hexadecimal YEY !!
